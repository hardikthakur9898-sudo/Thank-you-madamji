Where Everything Started

It was such an ordinary object — Booklet No. 03, Electrostatics, Current Electricity, MEEC — a little worn at the edges, filled with formulas nobody looks twice at. And yet, somehow, that Physics booklet is the reason this page exists at all.

I didn't know, when I asked to borrow it, that I was about to meet someone I'd think about for a very long time. No dramatic moment, no sign in the sky — just a booklet passed between two people who had no idea what was starting.

Funny how the smallest, most unremarkable things end up marking the beginning of the chapters we remember the most.

A Letter

Dear Madam Ji,

I'm not writing this to ask for anything. Not another chance, not a reply, not a reconsideration. I just wanted a place where I could say, properly, what I've been meaning to say for a while now — thank you.

Thank you for a booklet lent between strangers that turned into conversations I looked forward to. Thank you for the version of myself I got to be around you — a little more open, a little more myself. Whatever we were, it mattered, and it made this stretch of my life better than it would have been without you in it.

I know things are what they are, and that's alright. People come into our lives and change them quietly, without needing to stay forever to have made a difference. You did that. I noticed, and I'm grateful for it.

I hope life is endlessly kind to you — the kind of kind you deserve. That's all this page is. No more, no less.

— Hardik ❤️
